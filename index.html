Core teaching beliefs

"I never teach my pupils, I only attempt to provide the conditions in which they can learn."

— Albert Einstein

I strongly believe a teacher is not someone who knows it all and has to pass their knowledge to their students, but rather a facilitator whose aim is to guide and provide an environment that supports and encourages learning. My goal is to set up circumstances where students feel motivated, engaged, and empowered to explore and discover knowledge themselves.

"Every student can learn, just not on the same day, or the same way."

— George Evans

Empathy is a skill we hear a lot about nowadays, and I believe it's essential for a teacher. Each student has a different learning style, background, motivation to learn, and personal challenges. Adapting the teaching style and, in part, content, is enriching for everyone involved.

"Students don't care how much you know until they know how much you care."

— John C. Maxwell

It's easier to learn when the content is relevant and meaningful to the learner. When a teacher shows they care by connecting learning to students' interests and up-to-date, real-life content, students are more likely to be engaged and motivated to learn. Because I am very curious and inquisitive myself, this doesn't come too hard to me.

"In learning you will teach, and in teaching you will learn."

— Phil Collins

Good human interactions always produce an exchange. Teachers share what they know, but students do too. As a result, both student and teacher grow. I am fortunate to meet very interesting people, which gives me a glimpse into their areas of business, often from different perspectives, as my students are at various steps of the corporate ladder.
